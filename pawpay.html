UX Case Study

Addressing the everyday pain points of payment app users and simplifying the user flows by 33%

SCOPE

Self Project

MY ROLE

UX Reseach, UI Design, information Architecture

TOOLS

Figma, Notion, Obsidian, Google Forms

Problem Statement

The UPI payment market in India is dominated by three major players - Google Pay, PhonePe and Paytm, all of which harbour flaws like:

  • Lack of UX psychology
  • Lengthy user flows
  • Cluttered interface & confusion
  • Poor ease of access

The Solution

PawPay reimagines the UPI payment app experience by optimizing user flows, strategically placing features, enhancing information architecture, and creating a clean, visually appealing design.

Research

Researching through a fair share of statistical websites online, I found that the UPI payment market in India is dominated by three major players - Google Pay, PhonePe and Paytm. Additionally, it was also found that the impact of UPI for Indians in their daily life is immense, consitituting to about 67.5% of the total transactions.

I then hopped onto Google Play Store to go through the reviews left by users for these apps, as it would help me understand the pain points and experience of everyday users. The key reviews which highlighted distinct issues were:

PhonePe

  • “Bloated.. too many densely packed options”
  • “Third party deals clutter the screen... it’s annoying”
  • “I use it mostly to scan QR and make payments, however the scan QR button is at the top & tiny”
  • “Difficult on the eyes sometimes... because of its colour & icon style...”
  • “Small font size...”

Google Pay

  • “Single scrolling page.. not organized properly”
  • “Can’t handle groups & splits separately”

Paytm

  • “Creating groups & splitting expense is a hastle”
  • “Quick access tools consists of tools which no ones needs to access quickly *_* “
  • “Recent chats, groups, history... a combined mess!”
  • “Ads and promotions occupy too much space”
