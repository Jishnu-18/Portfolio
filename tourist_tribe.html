Brand Redesign

Rebranding the travel agency Tourist Tribe with a new logo and website makeover

SCOPE

Self Project

MY ROLE

UI Design, Brand Identity

TOOLS

Figma, Adobe Illustrator

Objective

To rebrand the travel agency Tourist Tribe with a new logo, new color palette, and a redesigned landing page that improves the user flow and intrigues the users.

Breakdown of the Redesign

1. Logo Redesign

I started my rebranding endeavour by redesigning the logo. The old logo had a very poor color contrast. It also lacked adaptability. The redesign features a minimalistic approach with two color shades: a dark green logo for light background and a white logo for darker backgrounds. Additionally, it has two variants: an icon-only logo and a text-icon logo.

2. Hero Section

OLD

The hero section of the original webpage lacked any sort of highlighting of the brand. Furthermore, it failed to give the users an impression that it's a travel-based website.

REDESIGN

The redesign features a complete makeover - from the logo and nav bar to the search bar and background. The design descision have been briefed below.

The search bar with the logo atop gives the impression of a one-stop 'travel search engine'. The ability to apply filters is also added to improve functionality. They provide users with the ability to narrow down their searches and provides a hassle-free experience.

Categories have been placed near the search bar to help users who aren't sure about a particular place. It gives users a one-click option to explore areas of interests. The categories section in the old website looked outdated and was placed afar in a non-intuitive manner.

Meaningful use of the nav bar buttons were made in the redesign so as to facilitate intuitive interaction and easy access to actions like login and enquiry.

Top Destinations list provides a list of the most searched & visited places that are currently popular tourist hubs. This helps users make a decision easily if they're confused or are keen on exploring all the available options.

3. Feature - Ongoing Trips

OLD

REDESIGN

The new redesign emphasises on the exclusivity of the feature and improves the aesthetics with a Call-to-Action (CTA) button, a concise description of the feature, and a background image for a modern feel.
